Understanding College Debt

Student debt is an investment in your future, but without careful management, interest can compound and extend the life of the loan.

Federal and private loans function differently. Federal loans often offer income-driven repayment plans and forgiveness options, whereas private loans are strictly bound by the lender's terms. Understanding your principal balance, current interest rates, and loan servicer is the first step to taking back control.

Based on the analysis, here are your streamlined options to mitigate and eliminate your debt.

1. Income-Driven Repayment (IDR)

Caps your monthly payments based on your income and family size. Best for federal loans to prevent defaulting.

2. Refinancing

Consolidate multiple high-interest private loans into a single loan with a lower interest rate.

3. Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

If you work for a government or non-profit organization, your remaining balance may be forgiven after 120 qualifying payments.
